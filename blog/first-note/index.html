Why I am building a personal academic website为什么我要搭建个人学术主页

I do not want this site to be only a profile. I want it to become a place where my research questions, reading notes, projects, and unfinished thoughts can gradually accumulate. 我不希望这个网站只是一个简介页。我更希望它慢慢变成一个地方，用来沉淀我的研究问题、阅读笔记、项目记录，以及那些还没有完全成熟的想法。

The starting point 最开始的想法

The first reason for building this website is simple: I need a stable place to put things. In the past, many thoughts stayed in chat windows, notes, temporary documents, or scattered folders. They were useful at that moment, but after a few weeks I often could not easily find them again, and I could not see how one thought was connected to another. 搭建这个网站的第一个原因其实很简单：我需要一个稳定的地方来放东西。过去很多想法散落在聊天记录、临时笔记、文档和文件夹里，当时看起来有用，但过一段时间再回头，就很难找到，也很难看出一个想法和另一个想法之间是怎样连接起来的。

A personal website gives these materials an address. It does not need to be perfect from the beginning. What matters more is that it can keep changing with me. When my interests become clearer, the homepage can become clearer. When a project becomes more concrete, the project page can grow with it. When a vague thought becomes a research note, it can move from a private draft to a blog post. 个人网站给这些材料一个稳定的位置。它不需要一开始就很完美，更重要的是它可以随着我一起变化。研究兴趣变清楚了，主页就可以变清楚；项目推进得更具体了，项目页也可以一起更新；一个模糊的想法慢慢变成研究笔记时，也可以从私人草稿变成一篇 blog。

More than a CV 它不只是 CV 的网页版

A CV records results: education, papers, projects, skills. Those are important, but they are only the visible part. For someone still learning how to do research, the invisible part may be even more important: how I choose a problem, how I read a paper, how I get stuck, how I revise my understanding, and how a loose question slowly becomes a more precise one. CV 记录的是结果：教育经历、论文、项目和技能。这些当然重要，但它们只是可见的部分。对于一个还在学习如何做研究的人来说，不可见的部分可能更重要：我是如何选择问题的，如何读论文的，如何卡住的，如何修改自己的理解的，以及一个松散的问题如何慢慢变得更准确。

This is why I want the blog to exist. Blog posts can hold the middle part of research: not yet polished enough to be a paper, but already useful enough to record. A short reading reflection, a failed experiment, a proof idea that did not work, a question that keeps bothering me: all of these can become part of the site. 这也是为什么我想保留 blog。博客可以记录研究中间的过程：它们还没有成熟到可以写成论文，但已经有价值被保存下来。一篇短的文献阅读反思，一个失败的实验，一个没有走通的证明思路，一个反复困扰我的问题，都可以成为网站的一部分。

A place to make thinking more concrete 把想法写出来，本身就是一种整理

Writing for a public page forces me to slow down. If a sentence cannot be written clearly, it usually means that the thought behind it is still vague. If a research interest can only be expressed as a list of keywords, it means I have not yet understood the problem deeply enough. The website becomes a gentle pressure: it asks me to explain myself in a way that another person can follow. 写在公开页面上，会迫使我慢下来。如果一句话写不清楚，通常说明背后的想法还很模糊。如果一个研究兴趣只能被写成几个关键词，那也说明我还没有真正理解这个问题。网站会形成一种温和的压力：它要求我把自己的想法讲到别人也能跟得上。

I do not expect every post to be profound. Some posts may simply be records of confusion. But even confusion can be useful if it is written honestly and carefully. A clear record of where I got stuck may later become a clue for a better question. 我并不期待每篇文章都很深刻。有些文章可能只是困惑的记录。但如果这种困惑被诚实、仔细地写下来，它也会有价值。今天清楚地记录自己卡在哪里，可能以后就会变成提出更好问题的线索。

How I hope to use it 我希望以后怎样使用它

For now, I imagine three kinds of updates. The first is research notes: what I am reading, what I understand, and what remains unclear. The second is project logs: what I tried, what worked, what failed, and what I would change next time. The third is reflective writing: how I understand research training, communication, tools, and the habits that make long-term work possible. 目前我想象中的更新大概有三类。第一类是研究笔记：我在读什么、理解了什么、还有什么不清楚。第二类是项目记录：我尝试了什么、哪些有效、哪些失败、下次会怎么改。第三类是反思性写作：我如何理解科研训练、沟通、工具，以及那些支撑长期工作的习惯。

The long-term goal is modest but important: when someone opens this site, they should not only know my name and affiliation, but also get a sense of what I care about, how I think, what I am building, and what kind of questions I am learning to ask. 长期目标并不复杂，但对我很重要：当别人打开这个网站时，不只是知道我的名字和学校，也能大致感受到我关心什么、如何思考、正在做什么，以及我正在学习提出什么样的问题。
