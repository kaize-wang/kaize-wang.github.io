Paper depth: from phenomena to mechanisms 论文深度：从现象走向机制

Sometimes a paper has data, theory, and references, but still feels thin. I have been trying to understand where this feeling comes from. 有时候一篇论文并不缺数据、理论和文献，却仍然让人觉得“薄”。我最近一直在想，这种感觉到底来自哪里。

The confusing comment: “not deep enough” 那个让人困惑的评价：“深度不够”

“The content is there, but the paper lacks depth.” This is a comment that can be frustrating, because it sounds both clear and unclear. It is clear because the reader is not satisfied. It is unclear because the paper may already contain many things: background, literature, data, models, and conclusions. If all of these are present, what exactly is missing? “内容有了，但深度不够。”这句话听起来很明确，又很模糊。明确的是，读者确实觉得不满足；模糊的是，论文里明明已经有背景、有文献、有数据、有模型、有结论，那到底还缺什么？

At first I tended to connect depth with difficulty. A deep paper should use more advanced theories, more complicated methods, or a larger narrative. But this explanation is not satisfying. A paper can be technically complicated and still feel shallow. Another paper can use simple language and modest methods, yet feel insightful. So the problem is probably not only about complexity. 一开始我容易把深度和难度联系在一起：有深度的论文应该用了更高级的理论、更复杂的方法，或者更宏大的叙事。但这个解释并不充分。有些文章技术很复杂，却仍然显得浅；有些文章语言很朴素、方法也不夸张，却让人觉得有洞见。所以问题大概不只是复杂程度。

Description is only the first step 描述只是第一步

A lot of writing stops at description. It tells the reader what happened: a policy changed, a technology was adopted, a platform became more efficient, a group behaved differently. These descriptions are useful because they give the reader a place to stand. But if the paper stops there, the reader may still feel that the analysis has not really begun. 很多写作会停在描述层面。它告诉读者发生了什么：政策发生了变化，技术被采用，平台效率提高，某类主体的行为发生改变。这些描述是有用的，因为它给读者一个站立的位置。但如果文章停在这里，读者仍然会觉得真正的分析还没有开始。

The next step is to ask why. Why did this change appear? Why did it appear in this way rather than another way? Why did it work in one setting but not in another? Once these questions appear, the paper begins to move from description to explanation. 下一步是追问为什么。为什么这个变化会出现？为什么它以这种方式出现，而不是以另一种方式出现？为什么它在一个场景中有效，在另一个场景中却不一定有效？当这些问题被提出来时，论文才开始从描述走向解释。

Mechanism is a way to connect the middle 机制是在补上中间那一段

I used to think of “mechanism” as a formal academic word. Now I understand it in a more practical way: mechanism is the missing middle between a cause and a result. It answers the question of how one thing becomes another. 我以前会把“机制”理解成一个比较正式的学术词。现在更愿意把它理解得朴素一点：机制就是原因和结果之间缺失的那一段。它回答的是，一件事如何一步一步变成另一件事。

For example, saying that a digital tool improves governance efficiency is only a beginning. A mechanism-oriented analysis would ask: Did it reduce information delay? Did it change the division of labor? Did it make some actors more visible and others less visible? Did it shift decision power from one place to another? These questions make the phenomenon more concrete. 比如，说某种数字工具提高了治理效率，这只是一个开始。机制化的分析会继续问：它是不是减少了信息延迟？是不是改变了分工方式？是不是让某些主体变得更可见、另一些主体变得更不可见？是不是把决策权从一个位置转移到了另一个位置？这些问题会让现象变得更具体。

This is also where the paper’s depth begins to appear. The reader can see not only that something changed, but also how the change was produced. The paper no longer simply points at a result; it reconstructs the path toward that result. 论文的深度也常常在这里出现。读者看到的不只是“发生了变化”，而是能看到变化是怎样被生产出来的。文章不再只是指向一个结果，而是在重建通向这个结果的路径。

A possible workflow for deeper writing 一种更深入写作的流程

If I turn this into a writing process, it may look like this. First, identify the phenomenon and describe it clearly. Second, turn the phenomenon into a puzzle: why is it surprising, inconsistent, or worth explaining? Third, propose a possible mechanism. Fourth, ask what conditions make the mechanism stronger or weaker. Fifth, look for evidence that can support or challenge the mechanism. 如果把这个想法变成写作流程，大概可以是这样：第一，先识别现象，并把它描述清楚；第二，把现象转化成一个 puzzle，说明它为什么值得解释，为什么不是理所当然；第三，提出一个可能的机制；第四，继续追问什么条件会强化或削弱这个机制；第五，再寻找能够支持或挑战这个机制的证据。

This process is slower than simply writing down an answer, but it is probably more reliable. It prevents me from jumping too quickly from phenomenon to conclusion. It also reminds me that a good paper is not only a container of information, but a path of reasoning that the reader can walk through. 这个过程比直接写出答案要慢，但也更可靠。它能避免我太快地从现象跳到结论，也提醒我：一篇好论文不只是信息的容器，更是一条读者可以跟着走下去的推理路径。

A reminder to myself 给自己的提醒

When I feel that a paragraph is too flat, I should not immediately add more concepts. I should first ask whether I have explained the middle process clearly. What is the phenomenon? What is the puzzle? What is the mechanism? What evidence makes the mechanism believable? What boundary conditions should be admitted? 当我觉得一段写得太平时，不应该第一反应就是加概念。更应该先问：我有没有把中间过程讲清楚？现象是什么？问题意识在哪里？机制是什么？哪些证据让这个机制可信？又有哪些边界条件需要承认？

Maybe depth is not a mysterious quality. It is the result of patiently staying with the question for a little longer than the first answer. 也许深度并不是一种神秘品质。它只是意味着，在第一个答案出现之后，仍然愿意多停留一会儿，继续把问题往下问。
