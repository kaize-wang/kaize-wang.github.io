Research training under uncertainty 不确定性中的科研训练

This is a more personal note about the gap between what I want to do and what I am currently able to do, and about how to rebuild a concrete research process. 这是一篇更个人化的记录，关于我想做的事和当前能力之间的落差，也关于如何重新建立一个更具体的科研过程。

The uncomfortable mismatch 一种不太舒服的不匹配

Recently I have often felt a mismatch: the problems I want to work on are larger than my current preparation. This is not simply a matter of working harder. It is more like standing in front of a task and realizing that I do not yet have enough concepts, examples, techniques, or judgment to move through it smoothly. 最近我经常感到一种不匹配：我想做的问题，似乎比我当前的准备要大。它不是简单地“再努力一点”就能解决的，更像是站在一个任务面前，突然意识到自己还没有足够的概念、样例、技术和判断能力，无法顺畅地穿过它。

This feeling becomes especially clear when I revise a paper. On the surface, revision looks like changing sentences, adding references, or fixing proofs. But once I really enter the details, I find that every small change may require a chain of judgments: whether an assumption is reasonable, whether a theorem can be used, whether an error term is controlled correctly, and whether a proof route matches the problem. 这种感觉在改论文时尤其明显。表面上，修改论文像是在改句子、补文献、修证明。但真正进入细节之后会发现，每一个小修改背后都需要一串判断：假设是否合理，定理能否使用，误差项是否控制正确，证明路线是否和问题匹配。

The theoretical gap is not abstract 理论缺口并不是抽象的

For example, when I think about statistical error and approximation error, I quickly meet topics such as nonparametric estimation and statistical learning theory. I have seen terms like covering numbers, VC dimension, and pseudo-dimension, but seeing them is not the same as understanding how they work. I do not yet have a stable sense of when each tool should be used, what assumptions it needs, or how it changes once the setting is modified. 比如，当我思考统计误差和近似误差时，很快就会碰到非参数估计和统计学习理论。覆盖数、VC 维、伪维度这些词我都见过，但“见过”和“知道怎么用”完全不是一回事。我还没有稳定地理解每种工具适合什么时候用，需要什么假设，以及当问题设定发生变化时，它们会怎样变化。

This makes reading papers difficult in a very concrete way. I may find a lemma that looks relevant, but I cannot easily tell whether it can be moved into my own assumptions. I may see a proof technique, but I do not know which part is essential and which part is only convenient for that paper’s setting. In that moment, reading is no longer just reading. It becomes a test of judgment. 这会让读论文在很具体的层面变难。我可能看到一个看起来相关的引理，却判断不出它能不能搬到自己的假设条件下；我可能看到一种证明技术，却分不清哪一部分是本质的，哪一部分只是因为那篇文章的设定比较方便。这个时候，读论文就不只是读论文，而是在考验判断能力。

AI can help me understand unfamiliar terms and outline possible routes, but it cannot replace the final responsibility. If I cannot explain why a step is valid, then the step is not really mine. This is the boundary I need to keep reminding myself of. AI 可以帮助我解释陌生概念，也可以帮我梳理可能路线，但它不能替代最后的责任。如果我自己不能解释为什么某一步成立，那这一步就还不真正属于我。这是我需要不断提醒自己的边界。

What I lack is also a process model 我缺的还有一个“过程模型”

Besides theory, I also lack a concrete model of the whole research process. A finished paper is an outcome, but it hides the path that produced it. How was the problem positioned? How were references selected? How did the author decide which experiment was necessary? How did the proof develop from a rough idea into a formal argument? These are the parts I most want to see clearly. 除了理论，我还缺少一个关于科研全流程的具体模型。一篇完成的论文是结果，但它隐藏了生成这个结果的路径：问题是如何被定位的？参考文献是如何取舍的？作者如何判断哪些实验是必要的？证明又是怎样从一个粗糙想法变成正式论证的？这些才是我最想看清楚的部分。

If I only know the target but not the path, I may try to jump directly to a polished output. That creates pressure, and it also delays communication. I may keep working alone because the current version does not look good enough, but by the time it looks presentable, the direction may already have gone wrong. Research probably needs earlier, smaller, and more frequent feedback. 如果我只知道目标，却不知道路径，就很容易直接逼自己交出一个看起来完整的结果。这会制造压力，也会拖慢沟通。我可能因为当前版本“不够好”而继续独自修改，但等它终于看起来能拿出来时，方向也许已经偏了。科研可能更需要更早、更小、更频繁的反馈。

A research workflow I want to practice 我想练习的一套科研流程

A more concrete workflow may begin with problem clarification. Before searching for more papers, I should first write down what exactly I do not understand: is it a modeling issue, an identification issue, a proof gap, or an experimental design problem? Naming the type of problem may already reduce some confusion. 更具体的流程也许要从澄清问题开始。在继续找更多论文之前，我应该先写清楚自己到底不懂什么：这是建模问题、识别问题、证明缺口，还是实验设计问题？把问题类型说出来，本身就能减少一部分混乱。

Then comes literature mapping. Instead of collecting papers loosely, I should ask what role each paper plays. Some papers define the problem, some provide a proof technique, some offer an experimental template, and some serve as contrast. If I can assign roles to papers, the literature will no longer be a pile of PDFs. 然后是文献地图。与其松散地收集论文，不如问每篇文章在我的问题里扮演什么角色。有些文献负责定义问题，有些提供证明技术，有些给实验设计模板，有些则作为对照。如果能给文献分配角色，文献就不再只是一堆 PDF。

After that, I need to choose a technical route and write down its assumptions, even before the proof is complete. What do I need to show? Which existing result might help? Which assumption is the weakest part? Where could the argument fail? This turns proof writing from a mysterious activity into a sequence of checkable steps. 再往后，需要选择技术路线，并在证明完成之前就写下它依赖的假设：我需要证明什么？哪个已有结论可能有帮助？最脆弱的假设在哪里？论证可能在哪一步失败？这样一来，写证明就不再是一个神秘活动，而是一组可以检查的步骤。

Finally, communication should happen before everything is polished. A short note with the current question, attempted route, obstacle, and next plan may be more useful than a long document produced too late. This is also a habit I need to train. 最后，沟通应该发生在所有东西被打磨好之前。一份短说明，写清楚当前问题、尝试路线、遇到的障碍和下一步计划，可能比一份很晚才出现的长文档更有用。这也是我需要训练的习惯。

Learning under uncertainty 在不确定性里学习

The hardest part may be accepting that research does not feel like coursework. Coursework has chapters, exercises, and visible endpoints. Research often begins with not knowing what should be learned. This uncertainty makes me anxious, but it may also be the place where research ability is formed. 最难的部分可能是接受：研究并不像课程学习。课程有章节、习题和看得见的终点；研究常常从“不知道该学什么”开始。这种不确定会让我焦虑，但也许科研能力正是在这里形成的。

For now, I want to make the uncertainty smaller by making the process more visible. Write down the question. Map the literature. State the assumptions. Record failed routes. Ask for feedback earlier. Use AI for explanation and checking, but keep the core judgment in my own hands. 目前我想做的，是通过让过程变得更可见，来让不确定性变小一点：写下问题，画出文献地图，列出假设，记录失败路线，更早寻求反馈。AI 可以用来解释和检查，但核心判断要留在自己手里。

Maybe research training is not one sudden breakthrough. It is the slow building of a system that helps me keep moving when the next step is not obvious. 也许科研训练并不是某一次突然开窍，而是慢慢建立一套系统，让自己在下一步并不明显的时候，仍然能够继续往前走。
