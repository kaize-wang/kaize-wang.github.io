Research training under uncertainty 不确定性中的科研训练

A reflection on the gap between research ambition and research ability, and on how to rebuild a more concrete learning process. 一篇关于研究目标与自身能力之间落差的反思，也是在尝试为自己重建更具体的学习过程。

The first problem is a mismatch 第一个问题是能力与任务之间的不匹配

The most direct feeling I have recently is that what I want to do and what I am actually doing are beyond my current preparation. This mismatch makes research uncomfortable. It is not only a matter of effort, but also a matter of accumulation, judgment, and problem-solving ability. 最近最直接的感受是，我想做的事情、正在做的事情，和我当前的能力储备并不匹配。这种不匹配会让研究变得很吃力。它不只是努力程度的问题，更是积累、判断能力和解决问题能力的问题。

A simpler way to say it is: I still lack enough accumulation. I do not yet have a stable understanding of the field, and my grasp of many technical details is limited. When I need to judge whether a theoretical argument holds, whether a proof technique applies, or how an error bound should be controlled, I often feel uncertain. 更简洁地说，我仍然缺少足够的积累。我对领域发展的理解还不够稳定，对许多技术细节的掌握也有限。当我需要判断某个理论是否成立、某种证明方法能不能使用、某个误差界该如何控制时，常常会感到没有把握。

The theoretical gap becomes visible when writing 理论缺口在写作和证明时变得最明显

When revising a paper, I encounter many technical details. For example, when thinking about statistical error and approximation error, I realized that my understanding of nonparametric estimation and statistical learning theory is still shallow. I have seen concepts such as covering numbers, VC dimension, and pseudo-dimension, but I have not systematically learned their differences, relationships, or appropriate use cases. 在修改论文时，我会碰到很多技术细节。比如思考统计误差和近似误差时，我发现自己对非参数估计、统计学习理论的理解仍然很浅。覆盖数、VC 维、伪维度这些概念我都见过，但没有系统学过它们之间的差异、关系，以及各自适用的场景。

This makes reading papers passive. Different papers solve different problems and use different proof techniques. If I cannot judge whether a lemma or a method can be transferred to my setting, then I cannot really use the literature with confidence. 这会让读论文变得很被动。不同论文解决的问题并不统一，使用的方法也不完全相同。如果我无法判断某个引理或某种方法能否迁移到自己的假设条件下，就很难真正有把握地使用文献。

AI can help explain unfamiliar concepts, but if I do not understand the reasoning myself, I may only be following its answer. That is useful for orientation, but it cannot replace independent judgment. Research still requires that every important step be something I can explain and defend. AI 可以帮助解释陌生概念，但如果我自己没有真正理解推理过程，就可能只是被它的回答牵着走。它可以辅助定位问题，却不能替代独立判断。做研究仍然要求每一个关键步骤都是自己能够解释和承担的。

Research process is also something to learn 科研的“过程”同样需要学习

Besides knowledge itself, I also lack concrete examples of how research is done. How should I search and organize literature? How should I extract useful information? How should I design numerical experiments? How should I structure proofs and formulas? At what stage should I report progress? 除了知识本身，我还缺少关于“科研过程”的具体参照。应该如何检索和整理文献？如何提取有效信息？数值实验应该怎样设计？证明和公式应该怎样组织？推进到什么程度就应该汇报？这些问题都很具体，但我并没有形成稳定的方法。

A target is often easier to describe than the path toward it. If I only know what the final paper should look like, but do not understand how each part is produced, I may either force a result too early or rely too much on external tools. What I need more is a set of examples: papers to reverse-engineer, workflows to imitate, and feedback loops that correct mistakes earlier. 目标往往比路径更容易被描述。如果我只知道最后论文应该是什么样子，却不知道每一部分是怎样被做出来的，就容易过早逼迫自己给出结果，或者过度依赖外部工具。相比抽象目标，我更需要具体范例：可以逆向拆解的论文、可以模仿的工作流，以及能够更早纠偏的反馈机制。

Uncertainty changes the way learning feels 不确定性改变了学习的感受

Coursework has chapters, exercises, and clear endpoints. Research does not. I may not know what to read, what can be used, what should be excluded, how to modify an argument, or which proof route is appropriate. This uncertainty itself can be painful. 课程学习有章节、习题和明确终点；研究却没有。我可能不知道该读什么、哪些能用、哪些不能纳入、该怎样修改论证、应该采用哪条证明路线。这种不确定性本身就会带来痛苦。

Looking back, I also see my own problems. I sometimes waited too long because I wanted to produce something that looked complete before communicating. But delayed communication can turn small problems into long detours. It may be better to share incomplete work earlier, so that mistakes can be corrected before they become polished. 回头看，我自己也有问题。有时我总想先做出一个看起来比较完整的东西再沟通，结果反而错过了及时纠偏的机会。延迟沟通可能会让小问题变成长弯路。更好的方式也许是更早地暴露半成品，让错误在被打磨之前就得到修正。

A possible adjustment 一个可能的调整方向

My next step should be more concrete. First, I need to fill theoretical gaps with standard materials, even if the initial goal is only to build judgment rather than mastery. Second, I should reverse-engineer good papers instead of reading passively. Third, I should make communication more regular and treat AI as an assistant for explanation and checking, not as a substitute for reasoning. 接下来更实际的调整，可能有三点。第一，把理论缺口当作必须补的硬缺口，用标准材料系统过一遍，哪怕第一阶段的目标只是建立判断能力，而不是立刻精通。第二，少一些被动读论文，多做逆向拆解。第三，让沟通更有节奏，同时把 AI 限制在解释和检查的辅助角色，而不是让它替代核心推理。

Research training may be exactly this: gradually building the ability to ask better questions, judge arguments, and keep moving under uncertainty. 也许科研训练正是这样一个过程：逐渐建立提出问题、判断论证，并在不确定中继续推进的能力。
